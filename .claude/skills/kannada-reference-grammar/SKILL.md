# Kannada Reference Grammar (Book 07)

*ಕನ್ನಡ ಬರಹದ ಸೊಲ್ಲರಿಮೆ* (*Kannada Barahada Sollarime*) — D. N. Shankara Bhat's
4-volume descriptive grammar of written Kannada. The most comprehensive single
reference on Kannada grammar in the ettuge corpus.

---

## Why This Book Is Central

This is Bhat's magnum opus on Kannada grammar — 4 volumes (Vols 1–4 available,
Vols 5–7 not yet extracted), covering the entire language from phonology through
morphology to syntax, using entirely native Kannada grammatical terminology.

**Relationship to other skills:**
- `kannada-morphology`: the *application* layer (generate forms). This skill: the *reference* layer (understand why)
- `kannada-syntax`: draws on Books 25 + 07. This skill: Book 07 only, in full depth
- `old-kannada-grammar`: Book 14 (what's *wrong* with Sanskrit-based grammar). This skill: Book 07 (what *correct* Kannada grammar looks like)

## Chapter Reference

### Vol 1 — Morphology

**Ch 1 — ಮುನ್ನೋಟ (Preview)**
Grammar vs. vyakarana; speech vs. writing distinction; writing system, sandhi, sentence overview.
`book/vol1/kn/ch1`

**Ch 2 — ಹೆಸರುಪದಗಳ ಇಟ್ಟಳ (Noun Structure)**
11 sandhi rules; 16+ noun-deriving suffixes (-ike, -ta, -a, -vaLi, ...); compound nouns.
`book/vol1/kn/ch2`

**Ch 3 — ಉಳಿದ ಪದಗಳ ಇಟ್ಟಳ (Other Word Classes)**
Causative *-isu*; 12 compound-verb light verbs (biDu, koDu, koLLu, hogu...); qualifiers (non-inflecting); loanword treatment.
`book/vol1/kn/ch3`

**Ch 4 — ಪದರೂಪಗಳ ಇಟ್ಟಳ (The 36 Verb Forms)**
23 finite forms (past/non-past × 9 agreement + 5 negative) + 13 non-finite:
- **modalEsaka** — prior-action converb ("having done X, then Y")
- **mArEsaka** — purposive converb ("in order to do X")
- **oDanEsaka** — simultaneous converb ("while doing X")
- Past/present/negative participials; verbal noun (-uvudu)
`book/vol1/kn/ch4`

### Vol 2 — Verb Usage + Noun Phrases

**Ch 5 — ಎಸಕಪದಗಳ ಬಳಕೆ (Verb Usage)**
Main (*tiruLesakapada*) vs. auxiliary (*neravesakapada*); tense reference; aspect with *iru*; modals.
`book/vol2/kn/ch1`

**Ch 6 — ಪದಕಂತೆಗಳ ಇಟ್ಟಳ (Noun Phrase + Semantic Roles)**
*pAngugaLu* (arguments); *mADuga* (agent), *paDuvaga* (patient); case semantics.
`book/vol2/kn/ch2`

### Vol 3 — Verbal Arguments (Ch 7–8)

**Ch 7** — Argument frames for transitive/intransitive/ditransitive verbs.
**Ch 8** — Argument alternations: causative (-isu), middle (-koLLu), reciprocal, agent suppression (*paDu* passive).
`book/vol3/kn/full`

### Vol 4 — Pronouns + Demonstratives (Ch 9–10)

**Ch 9** — Personal pronouns: nānu/nīnu/tānu/avanu/avaLu/adu + plural forms + case paradigms.
**Ch 10** — Demonstratives: i- (proximal) vs. ā- (distal) stems; definite/indefinite deixis.
`book/vol4/kn/full`
