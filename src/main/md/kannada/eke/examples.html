ಹಲ್ಮಿಡಿ ಶಾಸನ — Halmiḍi Inscription
The earliest known Kannada inscription (c. 450 CE), originally in Kadamba script. Kadamba script is a direct descendant of Brāhmī, developed a few centuries after Asokan Brāhmī — so while the Brāhmī rendering below is approximate, it gives a good sense of what the inscription looked like to a reader of Asokan Brāhmī. The original Kadamba letters are visually closer to Tamil Brāhmī than to modern Kannada script. Sanskrit loanwords are heavily present; aspirates fully preserved in Eke.

ಮಾಹೇಶ್ವರ ಸೂತ್ರಾಣಿ — Māheśvara Sūtrāṇi
Pāṇini's 14 phoneme-grouping sūtras, said to have originated from the beats of Śiva's ḍamaru drum — hence Māheśvara (of Maheśvara/Śiva). They enumerate all Sanskrit phonemes in a sequence that enables Pāṇini's pratyāhāra abbreviation technique: any contiguous group of phonemes can be named by its first member plus the it-marker (the final consonant, shown with virama) of the last. The it-markers themselves are not pronounced — they are metagrammatical labels only.

In Eke: vocalic ṛ = x, vocalic ḷ = q, velar nasal ṅ = G, palatal nasal ñ = Y.
