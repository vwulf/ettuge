## Description

This is D. N. S. Bhat's academic monograph on the Havyaka dialect of Kannada, published
as part of the Linguistic Survey of India series from Deccan College, Poona. Havyaka
(also called Havigannada, ಹವ್ಯಕ ಕನ್ನಡ) is the dialect spoken by the Havyaka Brahmin
community of the coastal Karnataka districts and the Western Ghats.

**Note:** This is distinct from the later popular Kannada booklet (folder 09), which
was written for a general audience. This 1971 work is a rigorous academic description
written in English for linguists.

## Significance

Published in 1971 — the same year as *The Koraga Language* — this marks Bhat's
early systematic documentation of South Dravidian dialects. His later work on
typology and case marking in world languages drew heavily on this foundational
fieldwork in Karnataka and Kerala.
