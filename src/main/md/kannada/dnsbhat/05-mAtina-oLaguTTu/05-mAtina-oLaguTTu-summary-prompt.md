### 10. Neurolinguistics — Language in the Brain (Parts 26, 27, 28)
- Human speech is possible because the food/air passage (pharynx) is configured differently in humans vs. apes — the descended larynx allows a wider range of articulation.
- Brain lateralization: the LEFT hemisphere controls language production and comprehension for most people (it controls the right side of the body).
- Damage to specific brain areas causes specific aphasias:
  - Broca's area damage → loss of speech production (but comprehension may be retained).
  - Wernicke's area damage → loss of comprehension (speech produced but meaningless).
  - Some patients can produce nouns only; others lose only verbs.
- Studies of brain-damaged patients reveal the modular structure of language in the brain.
- Motor patterns for speech articulation are stored in the brain; language involves both meaning-processing areas and motor-control areas.
