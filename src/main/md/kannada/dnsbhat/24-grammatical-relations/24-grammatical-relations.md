## Description

This is D. N. S. Bhat's argument against the universality of **grammatical relations**
(subject, object, indirect object) — a fundamental debate in theoretical linguistics.

The mainstream view (Chomsky's Government and Binding, Relational Grammar) holds that
grammatical relations like "subject" and "object" are universal to all languages.
Bhat challenges this using evidence from Dravidian and other language families.

## Key Argument

Bhat argues that:
1. Languages like Kannada do not have a "subject" in the Indo-European/Chomskyan sense
2. What looks like subject/object in Kannada is better described by **case** and **animacy**
3. The cross-linguistic evidence does NOT support grammatical relations as universals
4. Dravidian languages provide the key counterevidence
