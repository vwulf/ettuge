> **Why not `ಮಾಡುವೆ` (mADuve)?**
> `ಮಾಡುವೆ` (mADu + linking -v- + 1sg -e) is a valid literary and formal Kannada form, with paradigm-mates: *ಮಾಡುವೆ, ಮಾಡುವೆವು, ಮಾಡುವಿರಿ…* These come from an older -uva- participial-based conjugation still found in classical poetry and formal registers. Bhat's §5.1.1 uses the -utt- non-past marker throughout — giving mADuttEne for 1sg — and does not double-count the -uve alternates. The same applies to all regular verbs: kELuve, mADuve, barave etc. are valid but outside Bhat's 36-count paradigm.

## Rational / Non-rational split

Kannada's 3rd person splits by rational (ಉತ್ತಮ ಪ್ರಾಣಿ) vs non-rational (ಉಳಿಕೆ) — a binary Dravidian distinction, not Sanskrit's tripartite gender. In plural, masculine and feminine merge into one rational form (ಮಾಡಿದರು / ಮಾಡುತ್ತಾರೆ).

## ಒಳನೋಟ — Key Notes

**ಅರಿಕೆರೂಪ (imperative):** No tense, no gender. The 1sg and 3rd forms are identical (ಮಾಡಲಿ) — context disambiguates. 2sg/pl are the true commands.

**ಜೋಡಿಸುವ ರೂಪ (converbs):** Four types allow complex temporal chaining without finite verbs — a distinctively Dravidian strategy. ಮಾಡದೆ (without doing) is the negative converb, distinct from ಮಾಡದ (negative participial).

**ಉದು-ಹೆಸರು (verbal nouns):** Three-way tense distinction — non-past / past / negative — all taking full case suffixes. The past verbal noun ಮಾಡಿದುದು (*mADidudu*) is less common in speech but grammatically regular.

**ಇಕೆ-ಹೆಸರು:** Two forms only — non-past and negative. No past ike-noun (*ಮಾಡಿದಿಕೆ does not exist).

**ಸೇರಿಸುವ ರೂಪ:** ಮಾಡಿಕೊಳ್ಳು (do-for-oneself) — the reflexive/benefactive uses the prior converb + ಕೊಳ್ಳು. This is a productive pattern across all Kannada verbs.

**Why not `ಮಾಡಿಹನು` (mADihanu), `ಮಾಡಲಾರೆ` (mADalAre), and similar?** — These are **compound verb forms** (ಕೂಡಿಕೆ ರೂಪ), not entries in the simplex paradigm:
- `ಮಾಡಿಹನು` = prior converb `ಮಾಡಿ` + emphatic/concessive particle `ಹನು` — a clitic that attaches to any converb of any verb, yielding "having done (I assert / contrary to expectation)". `ಹನು`/`ಹಾನು` is independent; it does not fuse morphologically with the verb stem.
- `ಮಾಡಲಾರೆ` = purpose converb `ಮಾಡಲು` + defective auxiliary `ಆರು` (1sg `-ಆರೆ`) → "I cannot / am unable to do". `ಆರು` is a separate verb used almost exclusively in negative modal constructions (literary/formal).

Bhat's 36 captures only **simplex forms** — one suffix applied directly to the verb stem. Compound forms are productive by multiplication: each of the 13 non-finite forms can combine with dozens of particles and auxiliaries (ಹನು, ಬಿಡು, ಹೋಗು, ಬರು, ಆರು, ಕೊಳ್ಳು…). Those ಕೂಡಿಕೆ constructions are documented separately — notably the vector verb (ನೆರವೆಸಕ) system covered in DNS Bhat Book 32.
