## Rational / Non-rational split

Kannada's 3rd person splits by rational (ಉತ್ತಮ ಪ್ರಾಣಿ) vs non-rational (ಉಳಿಕೆ) — a binary Dravidian distinction, not Sanskrit's tripartite gender. In plural, masculine and feminine merge into one rational form (ಮಾಡಿದರು / ಮಾಡುತ್ತಾರೆ).

---

## ಒಳನೋಟ — Key Notes

**ಅರಿಕೆರೂಪ (imperative):** No tense, no gender. The 1sg and 3rd forms are identical (ಮಾಡಲಿ) — context disambiguates. 2sg/pl are the true commands.

**ಜೋಡಿಸುವ ರೂಪ (converbs):** Four types allow complex temporal chaining without finite verbs — a distinctively Dravidian strategy. ಮಾಡದೆ (without doing) is the negative converb, distinct from ಮಾಡದ (negative participial).

**ಉದು-ಹೆಸರು (verbal nouns):** Three-way tense distinction — non-past / past / negative — all taking full case suffixes. The past verbal noun ಮಾಡಿದುದು (*mADidudu*) is less common in speech but grammatically regular.

**ಇಕೆ-ಹೆಸರು:** Two forms only — non-past and negative. No past ike-noun (*ಮಾಡಿದಿಕೆ does not exist).

**ಸೇರಿಸುವ ರೂಪ:** ಮಾಡಿಕೊಳ್ಳು (do-for-oneself) — the reflexive/benefactive uses the prior converb + ಕೊಳ್ಳು. This is a productive pattern across all Kannada verbs.
