# Karate — a personal series

This is an ongoing series of articles on my practice of Matsubayashi-ryū karate and kobudō. The pieces are both reference-oriented (kata attribution, terminology, training structure) and reflective (teachers, lineage, how the practice connects to the rest of life). The series grows as I work through my own understanding, so some pieces will appear before their companions.

I practice at Karate Sunnyvale under Sensei Jordan Du Val, with Sensei Stephen Trombley in the broader line. My training dates to 2008; the writing here is the longer-term attempt to understand what I've been doing.

## A note on sources

Where I cite Nagamine, I mean *The Essence of Okinawan Karate-Dō* (Charles E. Tuttle, 1976), translated by Shinzato Katsuhiko. Where I cite historical karate research, I mean Andreas Quast's *A Stroll Along Ryukyu Martial Arts History* and related work, and Patrick McCarthy's *Bubishi* and *Tales of Okinawa's Great Masters* (the latter his translation of Nagamine's second book). Where I cite my teachers, I draw on seminar teaching, email exchanges, and personal instruction over the years.

Anything that looks mistaken is mine. Corrections are welcome.
